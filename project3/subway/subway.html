Keith Haring was known for using the city as his canvas, making chalk drawings in subway stations.
Keith Haring was internationally recognized as a public artist and social activist.
Keith believed graffiti spoke of a world that was hip and streetwise, creative and spontaneous and underground.
Keith was born on May 4, 1958, in Reading, Pennsylvania.
Keith moved to New York City in 1978.
Keith Died of AIDS-related complications on February 16, 1990, at age 31.
